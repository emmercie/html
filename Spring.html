Beef Bolognese

Home Summer Fall Winter

Beef bolognese is a very simple dish that can be made in a few hours.

  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 pound of ground pork
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Romano cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 pound spaghetti or your pasta of choice

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables are soft and the onion turns translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the ground beef and ground pork to the pot. Cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, red wine, beef broth, dried basil, dried oregano, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer, partially covered, for at least 1 hour. The longer it simmers, the more flavorful it becomes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. While the sauce is simmering, cook the spaghetti (or pasta of your choice) according to the package instructions. Drain.
  6. Remove the bay leaf from the sauce and taste for seasoning. Adjust salt and pepper if needed.
  7. Serve the beef bolognese sauce over the cooked spaghetti. Top with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh basil for garnish.
  8. Enjoy your homemade beef bolognese with a side of garlic bread and a sprinkle of additional Parmesan cheese, if desired.
